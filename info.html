IP Address: What is it and why does it matter?

A complete guide to Network Identification and Online Privacy.

What is an IP Address?

An IP (Internet Protocol) address is a unique numerical label assigned to each device connected to a computer network. It acts like your home address on the internet, allowing data to know exactly where it needs to be sent.

Difference between IPv4 and IPv6

There are two main types of IP addresses in use today:

  • IPv4 (Internet Protocol version 4): The most common format, consisting of four numbers separated by dots (e.g., 192.168.1.1). It is limited to approximately 4.3 billion addresses.
  • IPv6 (Internet Protocol version 6): Created to solve the IPv4 address shortage, it uses a much longer hexadecimal format, allowing for trillions of new addresses and enhanced native security.

Security Tip: Your public IP address reveals information about your Internet Service Provider (ISP) and your approximate geographic location (city and country).

Why monitor your IP?

Knowing your IP address is essential for several technical and security tasks:

  • VPN Verification: Confirming if your IP address has successfully changed after connecting to a VPN.
  • Network Configuration: Setting up servers, online gaming, or remote access.
  • Fraud Prevention: Identifying suspicious access attempts to your network.

How to protect your online privacy

Since your IP is a digital identity, many users choose to hide it to avoid advertising tracking or geographic censorship. Using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) is the most effective way to encrypt your traffic and replace your real IP with a secure server's IP.
